Frequently Asked Questions

DOES "SAN JUAN ISLAND" REFER TO THE ISLAND ITSELF OR A GROUP OF ISLANDS?

San Juan Island refers to an individual island in a chain of islands collectively known as the San Juan Islands. San Juan Island is the largest one in the archipelago, with the main incorporated town being Friday Harbor. The other two major islands in the island chain are Orcas Island and Lopez Island. Both are lovely with their own unique charms. Ferries run between San Juan Island and the other two islands daily. Feel free to reach out if you need any suggestions for what to do/see on the other islands.

ARE CHILDREN WELCOME?

That being said, there are a ton of family-friendly activities on San Juan Island, and there is definitely childcare available, so we would still encourage you to make a longer family vacation out of it!

HOW DO I GET THERE?

Planes, boats, and automobiles... there are many options available to you. In the words of the San Juan Islands Tourism Board, getting here is half the fun! See the Travel page of our website for in-depth descriptions of the most popular methods of transportation.

WHAT WILL THE WEATHER BE LIKE?

Summer is the best time of the year to visit the Pacific Northwest. The San Juans boast beautiful, sunny (and non-humid!) weather almost every day in summertime, with average daytime temperatures in the low-mid 70s and nighttime temperatures in the low-mid 50s in August. As you can see, it does cool off at night, so be sure to bring pants/sweaters/light jackets in preparation.

WHAT IS THE DRESS CODE FOR THE WEDDING?

The dress code for the wedding is cocktail attire - so suits and ties for the men, and cocktail dresses for women. FYI - for those planning to wear heels, the ceremony will be on grass at Roche Habor Resort's Sunken Garden. As mentioned above, it does get a little chilly at night, so plan accordingly.

The wedding ceremony is happening outdoors in the Sunken Garden at the center of the resort. The cocktail hour will be in the Pavilion Courtyard and the reception in the Pavilion building itself, which are located right across the cobblestone walkway from the ceremony site next to the Hotel de Haro. There will be signage to help guide you. Click here for a map of the resort, with both the ceremony and cocktail hour/reception sites starred. Roche Harbor's Guest Services team also provides courtesy golf carts chauffeurs to come pick you up from your resort accommodation if you don't feel like walking.
