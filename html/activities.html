Activities

We have always envisioned this weekend as a wedding ceremony tucked into a great vacation for our friends and family, and can't wait to share this special place with you all. The San Juan Islands are all about rugged natural beauty and majestic water views, and have long been known as a top destination for boating enthusiasts, nature lovers, wine and spirits connoisseurs, and foodies alike. We want you to have an amazing time on your island getaway! See below for just a few of our favorite things to do on the island.

For additional activities suggestions, see the Official Travel Site for the San Juans. For restaurant suggestions, find a comprehensive dining guide list for Friday Harbor here.

See The Sights

LIME KILN POINT STATE PARK

A favorite spot on the island to watch the sunset! Set on a rocky bluff at the west end of San Juan Island, Lime Kiln Point is considered one of the best whale-watching spots on earth. You can also hike, bird watch, and tour the historic lighthouse.

SOUTH BEACH

Far away from Miami's version, this South Beach possesses a different kind of beauty. It is the longest public beach in the islands, with views over the Strait of Juan de Fuca to the Olympic Mountains. Take a stroll along the water or relax around a firepit.

CATTLE POINT LIGHTHOUSE

Arguably one of the most beautiful places on the island. Built in 1935, this lighthouse stands on a lovely, windswept area of grassy dunes. A short trail takes hikers to the lighthouse and down to the glacier-carved rocks at the water's edge.

Out On The Water

WHALE WATCHING

San Juan Island is known as one of the best places to see orca whales in the lower 48 (not to mention bald eagles, seals, sea lions, and other wildlife). A can't-miss activity for your trip!

BOATING

The San Juans are a world-renowned boating destination. A variety of motorboats and sailboats are available for rental or private charter, many of which are conveniently docked at Roche Harbor.

SEA KAYAKING

Kayak trips from San Juan Island offer unobscured views of the Olympic Mountains. You might even see an Orca! Many of these rental companies are based out of Roche Harbor.

FISHING

The San Juan Islands provides some very scenic fishing opportunities during the summer on bottomfish and returning Chinook, sockeye, and pink salmon to Puget Sound and the Fraser River.

Keep Your Feet Dry

HORSEBACK RIDING

San Juan Island offers incredible scenery, which can be experienced from horseback. Horseshu Ranch offers trail rides and horseback riding lessons appropriate for any skill level, allowing visitors to view and experience San Juan Island in the best way. Great for families.

ZIP LINING

San Juan Island Zip Tour has 8 ziplines and 1 suspension bridge. Their first few ziplines will start out low and simple, with guests 10 – 20 feet off the ground. But once the tour journeys deeper into the forest, you’ll start seeing longer, higher and faster lines with one even riding over a lake.

GOLF & TENNIS

Open to the public, the San Juan Golf & Tennis Club features a challenging, 9-hole, links-style course that has been rated one of the best 9-hole courses in the region by the Pacific Northwest Golf Association. Also offers a driving range and a full-service restaurant.

Relax and Unwine

SAN JUAN VINEYARDS

Founded in 1996 by Yvonne Swanberg and her late husband, Steve, San Juan Vineyards is a producer of award winning estate-grown white varietals, Siegerrebe and Madeleine Angevine. Come do a tasting on their historic 6-acre estate.

SAN JUAN ISLAND BREWING CO.

Located in downtown Friday Harbor, San Juan Island Brewing Co. is an independent, local craft brewery. Also serves wood stone-fired pizza, burgers, and other pub style food. Come enjoy a beer and some grub on their beautiful patio!

SAN JUAN ISLAND DISTILLERY

Located less than a mile from Roche Harbor, San Juan Island Distillery makes small batch spirits, including 12 different gins, several liqueurs, and flavored brandies, including their award-winning apple brandy. All are available for tastings.

WESTCOTT BAY CIDER

The sister operation to San Juan Island Distillery. Westcott Bay Cider is the second-oldest ciderworks in Washington. They harvest many varieties of cider apples in their orchard and turn them into traditional English-style hard ciders.

Farm To Table

WESTCOTT BAY SHELLFISH FARM

A great spot to eat and drink on the island! See aquaculture farming in action on the beaches while shucking and slurping your freshly-caught oysters and enjoying a cold beer at picnic tables right on the water. Additional food and drinks available to purchase.

PELINDABA LAVENDER FARM

Pelindaba Lavender Farm is spread over 25 beautiful acres of lavender fields with lake and Olympic Mountain views. More than 200 lavender-based botanical, culinary, therapeutic, hygiene, and pet care products are handcrafted and sold at their on-site gift shop.

KRYSTAL ACRES ALPACA FARM

Krystal Acres is an 80 acre farm where an ever-growing herd of over 50 alpacas enjoy sunny pastures and the shade of massive 100 year oaks. Stroll around the farm, then browse an extraordinary selection of alpaca sweaters, coats, and shellcuddly alpaca toys in their gift shop.

Get Up And Go

MOPEDS & COUPES

Explore all the island has to offer at your leisure with a rental moped, scoot coupe, electric bike or car from San Juan Island’s Susie’s Mopeds. Pack your lunches and enjoy the spectacular scenery of a full-day tour or just rent at an hourly rate.

SEA PLANES

Though sea planes are a valid form of transportation unto themselves, these same airlines also offer scenic tours. Soar past the summit of Mount Constitution, and cruise the coasts of the other 171 islands that make up the San Juans.

BICYCLING

San Juan Island is the place for some good intermediate cycling. There is a mix of gentle roads, some grueling hills, and of course, beautiful scenery. Bring your own or rent bikes in Friday Harbor, a short walk from the ferry.

JOLLY TROLLEY

The Friday Harbor Jolly Trolley invites you to join us on a fun, guided exploration of the attractions on San Juan Island. Traveling on the beautiful Scenic Byway, their tours are narrated by informed guides with state-of-the-art audio and video.
